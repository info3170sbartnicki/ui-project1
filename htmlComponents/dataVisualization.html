Charts

Our charts are presented in a classic editorial style.

Line Charts

Notes

To make the data on the chart better readable, use horizontal and vertical lines.

Lines must include markers and be straight (2pts).

Layout elements: Legend (Right), Horizontal Axis Title, Vertical Axis Title, Horizontal Axis, Vertical Axis, Major Gridlines, Dropline

Bar Charts

Notes

Don't round corners.

The gap width should always be 150% and the series overlapped 100%.

For normal and horizontal charts, you should use color combinations similar to one of the previous moods.

Area Charts

Notes

The data in a normal area chart should be 50% transparent.

Drop lines are usually added to make it easier to keep track of time.

Scatter Plot

Notes

Markers should be of different shapes, 5px in size. Be sure to turn on the grid to make it easier to read the data.

Pie Chart

Notes

Pie charts are only recommended if you want to display one or more data series, all of which are non-negative.

Donut Chart

Notes

Avoid Donut charts whenever possible. The human mind thinks linearly, but when it comes to angles and areas, it becomes difficult for us to perceive and compare.

Palette

The colors for our charts are desirable to follow a single tone for visual aesthetics. When creating a chat, select one of the proposed color combo categories.
